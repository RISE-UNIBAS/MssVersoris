Aarau_AKB_MsWettF42

Identifier: Aarau, Aargauer Kantonsbibliothek, MsWettF 42
Content
    Manuscript attribution: No attribution.
  • 1ra–99va
    QsP
    Mss relationships: QsP Is particularly close to Basel F III 10
  • 100ra–102ra
    Fragment of a commentary on DC
    Attribution: According to Lohr, the fragment of a commentary on DC at ff. 100ra-102ra is Versoris's text. However, neither the titles of the questions nor their content correspond to Versoris'commentary. The structure of these questions is indeed similar to that of Versoris’s commentary, with notabilia, conclusions and doubts.

  • 103ra–106vb
    Description: Expositio cum questionibus
    Mss relationships: Very similar to the DA commentary transmitted in ms. Basel F I 13: the questions transmitted are the same; the structure is the same; it could be a copy of this text.
    Paleographical note: Copied by a different hand than following text.
    • 102ra
      Utrum scientia de anima sit pars philosophie naturalis
    • 105va
      Utrum omnis notitia sit de numero bonorum honorabiliorum
    • 106vb
      Utrum scientia de anima sit de numero difficiliorum
      Note: Incomplete
  • 107–134vb
    QdA
    Paleographical note: Copied by a different hand than previous text.
    Note: Unfinished, up to q. II "Utrum sensus tactus sit unus vel plures"
  • After QdA
    Copulata super tres libros De anima Aristotelis iuxta doctrinam Thomae Aquinatis
    Note: QdA is followed by the edition of Lambertus de Monte's Questions on the same text (Hain 11583)
  • Before QdC
    Note: Cologne editions of QdC, QsM, QdG and QPN printed by Molner in 1485.
History
Date of origin: 1486
Place of origin: Cologne
The watermark P is similar to Piccard XII 81 and 155, both used in Cologne in 1486. This dating and origin could be confirmed a) by the date of the editions inserted in the codex, all printed in 1485; b) by the fact that the ms. contains, above Versoris’s text, Lambertus de Monte’s Questions on De anima, a thomistic commentary used in Cologne.

Siena_Intronati_H-VI-7

Identifier: Siena, Biblioteca Comunale degli Intronati, H.VI.7
Content
  • 1r–56r
    CsPH, II-III
    Note: On f. 1v, we find a list of Italian mottos, beginning with this one: "Chi vuole de ogni cossa essere vincente / cum humilta se trouve patiente / chi spende piu che soa borsa non rende / non se l’abia amare se poverta lo prende". After this list, we find a note on Albert the Great’s conception of universals: "nota, secundum Albertum, quod universale est triplex, scilicet ante rem, post re et in re. Universale ante rem est ipsa prima causa, comprehendens omnia tanta potentialiter in se ipsa; universale in re est ipsa natura communis induta aliqua materia et quantitate, particularizata in hoc et in hoc subiecto; universale post rem est ipsa natura terminis denudata a tali materia et quantitate subiecti que, postea apprehenditur ab intellectu per conceptum generis et speciei, habet formare de ipso qui postea conceptus, iunctis primis vocatur ‘secunda intentio’. Nota etiam: universale habet duplex esse, scilicet formale et materiale. Esse formale et obiective est quando habet esse in intellectu tantum; esse materiale et fundamentale est esse in suius singularibus".
    Note: On f. 56rb, a short sentence schematically represents the Aristotelian categories: "Aristoteles (sup. lin.: id est, substantia), magnus (quantitas), Philosophus (qualitas), Sifronici filius (relatio), disputando (actio), fatigatus (passio), in scolis (ubi), hodie (quando), sedet (situatus) coronatus (habitus)".
    Attribution: Transmitted anonymously, but the colophon at f. 25ra specifies its Parisian origin: "Secundum est de predicamentis Aristotelis, secundum lecturam parisiensem".

Composite manuscript, copied in the Dominican convents of Pavia and Milan, as evident from the colophons of the friars Petrus Iohannes Balbus and Franciscus de Rippa.
History
Date of origin: 1458
Place of origin: Milan, Dominican convent Pavia, Dominican convent
The Dominican provenance of the codex is confirmed by the illumination at the beginning of the second treatise of the commentary on Petrus Hispanus (f. 2r), where St. Dominic is portrayed with a black mantle and a white tunic.Most of the texts in this manuscript are of Parisian origin.For the Italian didactic context of the confection of the volume, see f.1v.The possession mark, on a piece of parchment from the original lost guard, states that the part of the volume containing the logical commentaries belonged to Petrus Iohannes Balbus, who copied it in 1458: "Hoc commentum super artem veterem et philosofiam est mei, fratris Petri Iohannis Balbi Ianuensis ordinis Predicatorum, quod scripsi in diversis temporibus et ipsum finivi anno Domini M°CCCCLVIII° die Va iulii. Et omnia que in ipso continentur fuerunt extracta a libello valde incorrepto". On other old fragments, now pasted on the inner front cover, we read: "Iohannis Balbi. Commentum supra philosophiam Aristotelis"; "Commentum supra philosophiam Iohannis Balbi fratris ordinis predicatorum. [a. m.] 1458".The watermark, a flower, is similar to Piccard XII, II 967 (Pavia, 1448).
